Our Story

We grew up just a few miles apart, yet somehow our paths never truly crossed until 2015 and it wasn’t until 2020 that we finally found our way to each other.

Starting a relationship in the middle of lockdown probably wouldn’t feature in any dating guide. But a picnic two metres apart at Summer Leys Nature Reserve soon became long walks around Wilby and Earls Barton and endless chats on park benches.

What Alicia didn’t know was that Tom had noticed her years earlier in the Sixth Form common room, when she was the new girl at school. He added her on Facebook and, in classic Tom fashion, nervously waited four years before ever sending a message.

Since 2020, we’ve made a home together, travelled to eight countries, navigated a few job changes, over 20 trips to the theatre and shared more bottles of Prosecco than we’d care to count.

In March 2025, Tom proposed on the same bench where we’d had our first date. Alicia’s first words were, “NO WAY!” but of course it was always going to be a yes.

Now, we can’t wait to celebrate our wedding surrounded by the people who mean the most to us.

We’d love you to join us on Saturday 5th September 2026 as we begin the next chapter of our lives together.

Travel & Accommodation

Getting There

By Car

We recommend that you park at Harrowden Hall for the whole day as there is limited parking in Earls Barton. Please use the bus provided that will take you to the church and return you to Harrowden Hall after the ceremony. Please tell us if you intend to use this option when you complete the RSVP form.

If you require accessible parking at All Saints Church, please let us know when you RSVP.

Please note the bus from Harrowden Hall will leave promptly at 12:15 pm.

By Train

The nearest station is Wellingborough, which is approximately a 10-minute taxi ride from Harrowden Hall. There are also train stations at Northampton and Kettering.
